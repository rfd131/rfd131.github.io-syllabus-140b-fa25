Important: Your final grade is determined by four factors:
  • Number of Learning Targets marked as Demonstrated and Proficient
  • Number of written homework assignments that are Satisfactory
  • Number of lab assignments that are Satisfactory
  • Experience points (XP) earned through online work and participation

Your grade is not a percentage and you do not lose points by attempting assignments. Every assignment is a learning opportunity.

Learning Target Quizzes

There are 25 Learning Targets in the course. You are assessed on these through weekly Learning Target Quiz Sessions every Tuesday beginning in Week 2.

How Learning Target Quizzes Work

  • Grading: Success/Not Yet basis
  • Success: Free from conceptual errors with few or no minor computational errors
  • Not Yet: Shows that additional skill development or conceptual understanding is needed

Demonstrated: Earn your first Success on a learning target
Proficient: Earn a Success on a target you've already Demonstrated

Important for LT1-LT10: After a learning target is offered twice, you must visit LA or instructor office hours before additional attempts.

Experience Points (XP) Modifications

XP can modify your final grade by increasing your count of Demonstrated targets, Proficient targets, or Satisfactory written homeworks.

XP Modification Table

XP Earned Effect
Less than 100 XP No modifications
100-199 XP One modification
200-299 XP Two modifications
300+ XP Three modifications

Earning XP (approximately 350 XP available)

  • Online Homework: 10 XP for 80%+ (scaled for lower scores)
  • Pre-lecture Assignments: 5 XP for 80%+ correct
  • Weekly Check-in Surveys: 2 XP
  • LA Office Hours: 10 XP per session attended

Written Homeworks

10 assignments throughout the semester, graded Satisfactory/Not Yet/Incomplete

Written Homework Grading

  • Satisfactory: Submitted on time, organized, correct notation, correct computations, complete sentences
  • Not Yet: Submitted on time with reasonable effort but missing some criteria - revision allowed
  • Incomplete: Not submitted on time or insufficient effort - no revision allowed

Two exceptions allowed per semester via Google form in Canvas

Lab Assignments

4 labs throughout the semester requiring in-class participation and written report

  • Must participate in class to receive credit
  • Graded Satisfactory/Not Yet/Incomplete
  • Revisions allowed for "Not Yet" submissions
