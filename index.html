Welcome to Math 140B!

Welcome to Calculus with Applications to Biology. Throughout the semester we will focus on developing and understanding the tools of calculus and applying these tools to problems in biology.

Course Format: The Fall 2025 semester runs as a "3+1" hybrid course with 3 credits of in-person instruction (MWF) and 1 WEB credit corresponding to online asynchronous content.
Specifications Grading: This course uses a specifications grading scheme. Your grade is NOT based on points or percentages, but on proficiency in learning objectives assessed through Learning Target Quizzes, Written Homework Assignments, and Labs.
Feedback Loop of Learning

Course Overview

Prerequisites

  • Math 022, Math 026; or
  • Math 040 or Math 041 or equivalent

Class Structure

  • Monday, Wednesday, Friday: Interactive in-class sessions with direct instruction, group problem-solving, and review. Fridays include practice quiz time.
  • Tuesday Recitations: Required weekly Learning Target Quiz sessions (may be at different time/instructor than MWF section).
  • Online Components: Video lectures, pre-lecture assignments (PLAs), online homework, and self-guided learning activities.
  • Lab Days: Four lab sessions throughout the semester for hands-on mathematical modeling.

Assessment Components

  • 25 Learning Targets assessed through weekly quiz sessions and exams
  • 4 Writing Projects
  • Experience Points (XP) earned through:
    • Participation in LA Community Learning Sessions
    • Online homework (weekly)
    • Pre-lecture assignments (PLAs)
    • Participation in LA Community Learning Sessions
    • Online Practice Problems
    • Surveys
Note: Approximately 720 XP are available throughout the semester. XP can modify your final grade through earned modifications.
