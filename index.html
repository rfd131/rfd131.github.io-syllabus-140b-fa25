Welcome to Math 140B!

Welcome to Calculus with Applications to Biology. Throughout the semester we will focus on developing and understanding the tools of calculus and applying these tools to problems in biology.

Course Format: The Fall 2025 semester runs as a "3+1" hybrid course with 3 credits of in-person instruction (MWF) and 1 WEB credit corresponding to online asynchronous content.
Specifications Grading: This course uses a specifications grading scheme. Your grade is NOT based on points or percentages, but on proficiency in learning objectives assessed through Learning Target Quizzes and Writing Projects.
Feedback Loop of Learning

Course Overview

Prerequisites

  • Math 022, Math 026; or
  • Math 040 or Math 041 or equivalent

Class Structure

  • Class days (MWF): In-class days are interactive and include a mix of direct instruction, problem solving in groups, and review.
  • Tuesday Quiz Sessions: We have required weekly assessments on Tuesdays in Learning Target Quiz sessions.
  • Online: The asynchronous content includes online video lectures, pre-lecture assignments (PLAs), online homeworks, practice problems, and self-guided learning activities.

Assessment Components

  • There are 25 Learning Targets assessed through LT Quiz Sessions. Most LTs have two parts: a Foundational quiz and an Advanced quiz.
  • There are two evening midterm exams and a final exam. Each exam is essentially an extended quiz session with a larger packet of LT quizzes.
    If you meet the requirements for an A in the course by the end of Week 15, then you will not have a final exam.
  • There are four Writing Projects.
  • Experience points (XP) measure your engagement in Math 140B outside the classroom and contribute to your final letter grade through modifications. Participation in LA Community Learning Sessions, Completing Pre-lecture Assignments (PLAs), Online Homeworks, and Target Practice Assignments, and completing quiz sign ups and surveys all earn experience Points (XP).

General Education

Math 140B is part of Penn State's General Education curriculum and addresses the Key Literacies and Critical and Analytical Thinking general education objectives:

  • Key Literacies - the ability to identify, interpret, create, communicate and compute using materials in a variety of media and contexts. Literacy acquired in multiple areas, such as textual, quantitative, information/technology, health, intercultural, historical, aesthetic, linguistic (world languages), and scientific, enables individuals to achieve their goals, to develop their knowledge and potential, to lead healthy and productive lives, and to participate fully in their community and wider society.
  • Critical and Analytical Thinking - the habit of mind characterized by comprehensive exploration of issues, ideas, artifacts, and events before accepting or formulating a conclusion. It is the intellectually disciplined process of conceptualizing, applying, analyzing, synthesizing, and/or evaluating information gathered from, or generated by, observation, experience, reflection, reasoning, or communication, as a guide to belief and action.
