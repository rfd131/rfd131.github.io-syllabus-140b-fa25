Getting Help in Math 140B

Success in Math 140B comes from actively engaging with the material and seeking help when needed. We offer multiple resources to support your learning throughout the semester. Don't wait until you're struggling - use these resources early and often!

Remember: For the first 9 foundational learning targets (LT1F-LT9F), if you don't earn a Success during the first two attempts, you'll need to visit an LA or instructor office hour before attempting again. This requirement helps ensure you get the support needed to succeed.

üìö Guided Study Groups (GSG Sessions)

Peer-Led Study Sessions

Guided Study Groups are peer-facilitated study sessions designed to help you master course content through collaborative learning. These sessions are led by undergraduate students who have previously excelled in Math 140B.

What GSG Sessions Offer:

  • Review of key concepts from recent lectures
  • Practice problems similar to Learning Target Quizzes
  • Study strategies specific to Math 140B
  • A supportive environment to ask questions
  • Opportunity to learn from peers who recently succeeded in the course

Schedule Information:

GSG sessions typically meet 2-3 times per week. The complete schedule, including times and locations, will be announced on Canvas during the first week of classes.

GSG sessions are free and open to all Math 140B students. No registration required - just show up!

Professional Tutoring Services

Penn State Learning offers free tutoring for Math 140B and many other courses. This is an excellent resource for additional support beyond office hours and study groups.

Tutoring Options:

  • Drop-In Tutoring: No appointment needed - walk in during scheduled hours
  • Appointment Tutoring: Schedule regular weekly sessions with the same tutor
  • Online Tutoring: Virtual sessions available for remote learning
  • Exam Reviews: Special review sessions before midterms and finals

How to Access:

  1. Visit the Penn State Learning website: pennstatelearning.psu.edu/tutoring/mathematics
  2. Check the current semester's tutoring schedule
  3. Drop in during scheduled hours or make an appointment online
  4. Bring your course materials, notes, and specific questions

Location:

220 Boucke Building (Main Tutoring Center)

Pro Tip: Don't wait until you're struggling! Regular tutoring sessions can help you stay on track and deepen your understanding of the material.

üí° Tips for Getting the Most Out of Help Resources

  • Come prepared: Bring specific questions, attempted problems, and your notes
  • Start early: Don't wait until the night before a quiz to seek help
  • Be consistent: Regular attendance at office hours or study groups is more effective than cramming
  • Form study groups: Connect with classmates during LA sessions or GSG meetings
  • Use multiple resources: Different explanations can help concepts click
  • Review quiz feedback: Bring graded quizzes to office hours to understand your mistakes
  • Ask questions in class: Your instructors want to help - don't be shy about asking for clarification
