Grading Overview

Specifications Grading: Your grade is NOT based on points or percentages. Instead, your grade is determined by demonstrating proficiency in learning objectives through Learning Target Quizzes and Writing Projects, with modifications possible through Experience Points (XP).

Your final grade is determined by:

  • The number of Learning Targets marked as Complete (at least one part successful)
  • The number of Learning Targets marked as Proficient (all parts successful)
  • The number of Writing Projects that are Satisfactory
  • Experience points (XP) earned through online work and participation

Important: You do not lose points by attempting assignments. Every assignment is a learning opportunity!

You must meet ALL requirements to receive the corresponding letter grade. For example:

  • If you have 25 Complete targets, 15 Proficient targets, and 3 Satisfactory projects, your final grade would be B (not A)
  • If you have 23 Complete targets, 19 Proficient targets, and 2 Satisfactory projects, your final grade would be B+ (not A-)

Modifications earned through XP can help you meet these thresholds (see below).

Experience Points (XP)

Earning Experience Points

There are approximately 720 XP available throughout the semester. You earn XP through:

Lower scores are scaled

Target Practice 10 XP Complete target practice sets for Learning Targets
Weekly Surveys 2 XP Brief weekly reflection survey and quiz sign up

Note: Course engagement at a satisfactory level typically corresponds to earning about 400-500 XP (enough for 2 modifications).

XP Modifications

How Modifications Work

The number of learning targets Complete or Proficient, or the number of writing projects counted as Satisfactory, may be modified by experience points (XP) earned during the course.

Each modification earned will:

  • Increase the number of Complete targets by one, OR
  • Increase the number of Proficient targets by one, OR
  • Increase the number of Satisfactory writing projects by one
XP Earned Effect
Less than 200 XP No modifications
200-399 XP One modification
400-599 XP Two modifications
600 or more XP Three modifications (maximum)

Important: Modifications are automatically applied at the end of the course in the way that will best benefit your final grade.

üí° Example

A student finishes the semester with:

  • 22 Complete Learning Targets
  • 16 Proficient Learning Targets
  • 2 Satisfactory Writing Projects
  • 450 XP earned (= 2 modifications)

The 2 modifications could be applied to increase Proficient targets from 16 to 18, resulting in a final grade of A- (meeting the requirements of 22 Complete, 18 Proficient, and 2 Projects).

Note: Modifications are NOT applied to specific targets. For example, you cannot use one modification on a One-Time Proficiency target to increase both Complete and Proficient counts by one.

Math 197 Option (2 Credits)

Math 197: Differential Calculus with Applications to Biology

Prior to the late drop date (Friday, November 14, Week 12), you have the option to change enrollment to Math 197, a 2-credit course.

Passing grades in both Math 197 and the follow-on two-credit course Math 198 (Topics in Calculus with Applications to Biology) are together equivalent to a passing grade in the 4-credit Math 140B.

Understanding Learning Target Achievement

Complete vs. Proficient

To Complete a Learning Target:

  • Earn a Success on either the Foundational (F) Quiz Version OR the Advanced (Adv) Quiz Version
  • For One-Time Proficiency Targets, one success counts as both Complete and Proficient
  • Once you earn a Success in an LT Quiz Version, you do not attempt that version again

To be Proficient in a Learning Target:

  • Earn a Success on BOTH the Foundational (F) Quiz Version AND the Advanced (Adv) Quiz Version
  • For One-Time Proficiency Targets, one success counts as both Complete and Proficient
  • You don't need to earn a success in the Foundational version before attempting the Advanced version

See the Learning Targets page for more details about the quiz system.
