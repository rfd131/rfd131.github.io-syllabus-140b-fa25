Math 140B: Calculus with Applications to Biology - Fall 2025

Writing Projects

Writing Projects Overview

There are 4 writing projects throughout the semester. Writing projects involve exploring mathematical models related to biological systems and demonstrating your ability to apply calculus concepts to real-world problems.

What Are Writing Projects?

Each writing project focuses on a specific mathematical model and its biological applications. You will work through guided activities to:

  • Build and analyze mathematical models.
  • Apply calculus skills developed in class.
  • Make computations and interpret results.
  • Discuss the biological implications of your findings.

You'll submit a structured written report using a provided template that summarizes your work and demonstrates your understanding.

Grading Criteria

Writing projects are graded on a Satisfactory/Not Yet/Incomplete basis according to the following criteria:

Assessment Criteria

Completeness: The write-up addresses all of the prompts in the template.
Notation and Use of Symbols: The write-up demonstrates correct use of mathematical symbols and notation.
Interpretation: The write-up demonstrates the ability to communicate the key ideas and results.
Application: The write-up demonstrates an ability to apply the model explored to address a related question provided in the prompts.
Grade Criteria
Satisfactory
  • The project was submitted on time.
  • All assessment criteria have been met, with at most minor errors that don't impact the overall quality.
Not Yet
  • The project was submitted on time, AND
  • Demonstrates reasonable effort, BUT
  • One or more assessment criteria have not been met.
Incomplete
  • The project was not submitted on time, OR
  • A reasonable effort on the write-up has not been demonstrated.

Revision Policy

Opportunity for Improvement

If your writing project is graded Not Yet, you will have an opportunity to submit a revision after grading is completed.

How to submit a revision:

  • Review the feedback provided in Gradescope carefully.
  • Address all issues identified in the original submission.
  • Resubmit the revised project to the Writing Project Revision assignment on Gradescope.
  • Use the cover page to indicate which sections have been revised.
  • General Feedback and additional guidance will be provided after initial grading.

You are encouraged to discuss questions during instructor or LA office hours before submitting your revision.

Important: If your writing project is graded Incomplete, you will NOT have an opportunity to submit a revision. It is crucial to submit your work on time and demonstrate a reasonable effort on all parts.

Tips for Success

Best Practices for Writing Projects

  • Start early: Give yourself time to think through the problems and write clear explanations.
  • Use the template: The provided template ensures you address all required components.
  • Write in complete sentences: Mathematical work should be accompanied by clear explanations.
  • Check your notation: Proper mathematical notation is an important part of communicating your work.
